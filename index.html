Frequently Asked Questions

No, night safaris are not allowed inside Jim Corbett Park.

Yes, children under the age of two can go on the Jim Corbett Safari; there are no age restrictions, but parents must take extra precautions with their children.

Near Jim Corbett Park, there are many options for camping in the resorts, but inside the core area of Jim Corbett Park, camping is not allowed.

If we talk about attractions in the Jim Corbett National Park, here is a list of some of the animals you can see: Asiatic Elephants, Bengal Tigers, Leopards, Wild Boars, Sloth Bears, Sambar Deer, Hog Deer, Barking Deer, Jackals, Mongooses, Crocodiles, Gharials, Monitor Lizards, etc. The main flora of this park is Sal, Khair, Sheesham, Bel, Dhak, Semal, Rohini, Bakli, Chhabila, etc.

All the zones are good for Jeep safari. Dhikala, Jhirna, and Bijrani are the most popular zones of Jim Corbett Park.
